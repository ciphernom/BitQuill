How BitQuill Works

A revolutionary approach that restores the "physical properties" of authentic creation to digital documents

1

VDF Clock

A cryptographic timer produces sequential "ticks" that require real-world time to compute. These operations cannot be parallelized or accelerated beyond physical time constraints - even with specialized hardware.

How it works technically Verifiable Delay Functions (VDFs) are cryptographic constructions that require a specified amount of sequential computation. BitQuill uses Wesolowski's construction based on time-lock puzzles in RSA groups.
Learn more about VDFs
2

Paragraph Binding

As you write, each paragraph is cryptographically bound to the VDF clock's current tick, creating a verifiable record of the document's progressive creation over real time.

How it works technically Each paragraph's content hash is combined with the VDF output to create a commitment. This commitment scheme ensures the paragraph was created after the VDF tick was generated.
Learn more about cryptographic binding
3

Merkle Tree Construction

Document states are organized in a tamper-evident Merkle tree, where each node depends cryptographically on previous nodes, making retrospective manipulation immediately detectable.

How it works technically Merkle trees are cryptographic data structures where each parent node contains a hash of its children. This creates a tamper-evident history where changing any leaf requires recalculating the entire path to the root.
Learn more about Merkle trees
4

Multi-level Verification

The system offers multiple verification levels - from basic integrity checks to thorough cryptographic verification and forensic analysis of writing patterns.

How it works technically Verification can include: checking Merkle tree structure, validating VDF proofs, confirming commitment bindings, and analyzing the statistical properties of writing intervals.
Learn more about verification

BitQuill for Non-Techies

Understanding the value without the technical jargon

What's the Problem?

Imagine you've spent hours crafting a document — a legal brief, academic paper, creative manuscript, or important work project. Now imagine that anyone could claim they wrote it in seconds using AI. How would you prove your authentic effort?

That's the challenge we're facing today. With AI tools, it's becoming impossible to distinguish human-created content from machine-generated content just by looking at the final result.

How BitQuill Helps

BitQuill is like a digital notary that timestamps and authenticates your work as you write. It creates a tamper-proof record showing that your document was created progressively over time — the way humans naturally work — rather than generated all at once.

Think of it as adding physical "proof of effort" properties to digital documents, allowing you to demonstrate your authentic human contribution.

Simple Analogies

Like a Fingerprint

BitQuill creates a unique "fingerprint" of your writing process that's impossible to forge after the fact. Just as each person has a unique fingerprint, each authentic document has a unique creation pattern.

Like a Chain of Evidence

Similar to how police maintain a chain of evidence, BitQuill maintains a tamper-evident chain of your document's creation history, making it impossible to alter without detection.

Like a Time Capsule

BitQuill is like creating a series of time capsules as you write. Each paragraph is sealed with a cryptographic timestamp that cannot be backdated, proving when each part was written.

Key Features

BitQuill creates an unforgeable record of human document creation through advanced cryptographic techniques

Tamper-Evident Records

Any attempt to modify document history breaks cryptographic chains, making manipulation immediately detectable.

Unlike conventional version control systems where history can be rewritten, BitQuill's tamper-evidence comes from mathematical properties rather than trust. The Merkle tree structure ensures integrity of the entire historical record.

Time-Bound Verification

Mathematical proof that document creation required real-world time investment.

Verifiable Delay Functions create sequential proofs that a minimum amount of time has elapsed. These functions cannot be parallelized, ensuring that even with advanced computing resources, document creation represents real-world time investment.

Adaptive Security

Maintains consistent security across different hardware environments.

BitQuill automatically adjusts VDF difficulty parameters to maintain consistent timing relationships regardless of the underlying hardware. This ensures security remains effective as technology evolves.

Writing Pattern Analysis

Statistical verification of writing patterns typical of human authorship.

Natural human writing shows characteristic patterns: pauses between bursts of activity, variation in editing rates, and natural rhythms. BitQuill captures and analyzes these patterns to differentiate human authorship from machine generation.

Trustless Verification

No reliance on centralized authorities or external services.

Unlike systems that rely on timestamp authorities or blockchain networks, BitQuill provides completely local verification without requiring internet connectivity or trust in external services.

Exportable Proofs

Share compact verification data that others can independently validate.

BitQuill enables authors to share cryptographically secure proof files alongside their documents. These proofs allow others to verify the document's creation timeline without requiring the full historical record.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about BitQuill and human authorship verification

How does BitQuill differ from timestamps or blockchain solutions?

Unlike simple timestamps or blockchain solutions that only record when a document was submitted, BitQuill creates a sequential, fine-grained history of document creation. It captures the progressive development over time that characterizes human work, with cryptographic binding between content and time proofs.

Can't AI just simulate human timing patterns?

BitQuill uses Verifiable Delay Functions (VDFs) that require actual elapsed time to compute, regardless of computing power. While AI could attempt to mimic human timing, it cannot accelerate the VDF's sequential proofs. BitQuill also analyzes complex writing pattern statistics that are difficult to simulate convincingly.

How can I share verification proofs with others?

BitQuill allows you to export compact verification proofs alongside your document. These proofs contain the cryptographic data needed for others to independently verify your document's creation timeline without requiring access to the full historical record.

Is BitQuill open source?

Yes, BitQuill is completely open source under the GPLv3 license. This ensures that anyone can review the code, contribute improvements, or verify that the system works as claimed without any hidden backdoors or security vulnerabilities.

Can I use BitQuill with existing document editors?

Currently, BitQuill operates as a standalone editor, but we're developing plugins for popular document editors like Microsoft Word, Google Docs, and VS Code. These will enable BitQuill's verification features while letting you work in your preferred environment.
